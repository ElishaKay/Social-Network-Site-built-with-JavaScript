How To Measure Your Marketing ROI

In this article, I will take you through the fundamentals of online marketing.

Step 1 is to forget everything you already know.

The industry is changing at a rapid speed, with lots of new technologies coming out. Nonetheless, I believe that the fundamentals of Content and Distribution are the most important elements of any marketing campaign.





The 3 Rules of Advertising

Rule #1: It's not only about getting a ton of traffic. It's about getting the right traffic. Rule #2: It's much easier to be number 1 in your industry and stay there, than it is to be number 4, 5, or 10, and try to stay there.

Rule#3: Be able to measure the success of each of your traffic sources.


And guess what? With Google Analytics - you can! I don't work for Google Analytics (it's a free service). But, I use the service to manage my company's marketing budget.

Every time I invest in a new traffic source, I always log in to my Google Analytics dashboard in order to see how the source is performing. How many visitors did each source get me? How many of those visitors took the action I wanted them to take on the site - like, filling out a form, and applying for the program?

In this article, I would like to share with you what I think is the most important tab on your Google Analytics Dashboard. It is called the "Source / Medium" tab. In it, you can see the conversions you are receiving from every channel of your online marketing efforts. From this data, you can calculate the return on your investments, and use those insights to double-down on your most profitable channels, while cancelling the investments on sources that are bringing you crappy traffic.

Here is the 3-Step dance which will allow you to measure all of this, and more.

Step 1: Create Thank-You Pages on Your Website



Ask yourself:


what are the main actions I want people to take on my site?



Step 1 is to create "thank-you" pages when customers make a purchase on your site, fill out a contact form, or request more information for your services.

These thank-you pages are crucial because you need a "URL" to give your Google Analytics dashboard in order to measure the conversions, and profitability of every individual that visits your site.

A "Thank-You" page can also be the page that appears when another email subscriber joins your email marketing database. Visitors should be referred to these pages every time they complete a goal on your site.


Now that you have clarified your site's desired goals, and created the "thank-you" pages, you must plug in the "Conversion Goals" into your Google Analytics dashboard. Simply copy-paste the URL's of each "thank-you" page, and give each goal a title. Ba-bam. Now everything is all set up!

Step 3: Visit the "Source / Medium" Tab Frequently

------> This is the tab that shows the amount of traffic coming to your website from the top sources.
