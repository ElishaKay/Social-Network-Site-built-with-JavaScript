Pokemon Go and Big Data


We are entering a new era of User Comfort levels relating to online privacy. Trends like the "Pokémon Go" app are proof of this Sociological Shift within the Global Community of Mobile Phone users.

The app uses Google Maps in order to simulate an artificial hunt for Pokémon in the real world. Despite the users' comfort to share their GPS Location with the Game's Makers (and in response to the rapid adoption of the app - 7.5 million downloads in 5 days), the politicians are stepping in to issue grave warnings and capture a piece of the hype.

U.S. Senator Al Franken stated: "I am concerned about the extent to which Niantic [the Game's Maker] may be unnecessarily collecting, using, and sharing a wide range of users’ personal information without their appropriate consent.”

Personally, I believe that the over-obsession with User Privacy is non-sense! And I'll even tell you why.



Personalized Online Experiences

Fact #1: Facebook and Google have built their success around personalized online experiences.

For anyone that wasn't born yesterday, you'll realize that the debate over user privacy has been a hot topic for over a decade! The only thing is: any historian will clearly see that users become more callous over their personal privacy with each passing year.

Have a look at this article: "Facebook Users Revolt, Facebook Replies", published by TechCrunch on Sep 6, 2006 in response to the introduction of the Facebook News Feed and Personal Timeline (Yes! The very same features we have come to know and love).

The 2006 article states: "a Facebook group has been formed called “Students Against Facebook News Feed”. A commenter in our previous post said the group was closing in on 100,000 members as of 9:33 PM PST, less than a day after the new features were launched. There are rumors of hundreds of other Facebook groups calling for a removal of the new features."



Your Cellphone: The Only Drug That Learns How To Be More Addiciting Over Time

Fact #2: Facebook and Google are the only drugs that learn how to be more addicting over time.

The Evolution of The Average User's Publicity Comfort Level

Fact #3: "Pokémon Go" is simply the next step of the modern user's comfort level.

If you look at the big picture over the past 10 years, you'll realize one thing.

Users enjoy the lack of privacy!

So, please, as the world becomes more transparent, stop trying to figure out how to fight it.

Just ride the wave, dude!
