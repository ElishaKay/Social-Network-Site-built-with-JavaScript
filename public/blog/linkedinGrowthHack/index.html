The Best LinkedIn Growth Hack You'll Hear All Year

From my experience, the vast majority of companies and organizations do not utilize their email database properly.

Why? Because no one ever taught them how.

What I'm going to show you today is going to blow your mind. But before I do that, I want to show you a case study of 1 specific LinkedIn account that I took from having 148 connections on Monday, to 500+ connections today (Thursday).

Let's begin with the basics. On LinkedIn, you can create a profile as a company, or as an individual. I recommend creating a page for your company as an individual. Why? Because that way you can actually add contacts, and send them InMail's for free. With a company page, you can't do that for free. It sucks. (That's important lesson #1).

Okay, so once you create your individual page, and invite your friends from your personal email database, it may look something like this:


You've got your company logo as the profile photo, a nice cover photo, 148 connections, and you're feeling good in the hood, right? Nope. Let's develop that page!

Here's what that very same LinkedIn page looks like right now (within 4 short days):


Wow! How did that happen?! We've added a picture of Amy Smalls, our friendly internship advisor who sits in our offices. We also uploaded a bunch of emails from our emails database, and added them as her connections! She now has 500+ connections (nine of which asked her for an internship directly on LinkedIn).

Not only that! Amy started getting mail from all sorts of people (related to people in our database).

In this article, I will show how you can do the same thing for your company.

We will go through the steps of extracting the email list from your email database platform, and then adding that very same database of emails (an excel file) chunk by chunk unto your Linkedin network of connections. The process is simple.

Step 1

Log on to the website where you manage your database of emails. This could be MadMimi, Constant Contact, MailChimp, GetResponse, or whatever provider you use.

Step 2

Do a simple google search: "how to download my email database from __________ [INSERT NAME OF PROVIDER HERE]"

Step 3

The list of emails will probably be downloaded as a ".CSV" file - this is an excel file. Open the file and make sure that all of the emails are neatly categorized into 3 categories.


First Name Last Name Email Address
Joe Babaganoush jBaba@gmail.com
Steve Jackson BananaMan@aol.com

And, so on, down to the bottom of the excel file. Keep the list down to 2,000 names at a time (this is easy to measure in excel sheets). More info regarding proper format can be found here.

Step 4

Logon to your LinkedIn Account. When you're logged in, hover over the "My Network" tab, and click "Add Contacts."

Step 5

On the bottom-right, click the "Import file" button. Browse for the ".CSV" file you created earlier, and click "Upload file." LinkedIn will guide you through the rest of the process.


The Results



306 views for your brand is a nice bit of exposure, especially when you know the people checking you out have previously expressed interest in your business. After all, this strategy is based off of re-engaging leads that may have been unable to commit in the past, but may be happy to hear more today.


LinkedIn Limits


LinkedIn provides you with a maximum of 3,000 pending invitations. That means, if you have 10,000 leads in your database, your best bet is to break that list into 4 pieces, and have 4 separate employees upload 2,500 emails each via their individual LinkedIn Accounts.

Good Luck! And if you have any questions along the way, feel free to ask.
